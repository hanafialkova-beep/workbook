# The Future of No Work — Workbook
## For managers and leaders · From theory to practice

---

## 📖 About this workbook

This workbook is a practical companion to **The Future of No Work** by Filip Dřímalka. It is written for managers, team leads, and anyone driving AI adoption in teams and organizations. While Workbook 1 (“For everyone”) focuses on personal productivity, this workbook focuses on:

- ✅ How to map and use AI tools in the company
- ✅ How to systematically introduce AI into the team's work
- ✅ How to create tools and automation for the whole team
- ✅ How to work with specialized AI agents
- ✅ How to measure the success of AI adoption

### 🎯 Prerequisites

We recommend completing Workbook 1 (“For everyone”) first so you have hands-on experience with AI tools. This workbook builds on that and moves to the team and organization level.

### 📋 Exercise overview

1. **Map the AI tools in the business** — Find out what you have, what's missing and where the opportunities are
2. **Start AI adoption in the team** — A systematic plan for AI implementation
3. **Create an app or automation for the team** — A handy tool to make everyone's job easier
4. **Working with AI agents** — Specialized assistants for management tasks
5. **Automate repetitive processes** — End-to-end automation for the entire team
6. **Assess progress and plan** — Measuring success and next steps

## Exercise 1: Map the AI tools in the company

> **💡 Find out what AI tools you already have and what you're missing — you might be surprised.**

**⏱️ 30-45 minutes · Level: Manager**

**Goal:** Get a complete overview of the AI tools available to you in your company, identify gaps and find opportunities.

### What will you do?

You will map the existing AI tools in the business, identify who is using them (and who is not), and find the areas where AI can bring the most value.

### Why is it important?

Many companies are already paying for AI tools without fully utilizing them. Microsoft 365 Copilot, Google Workspace AI, GitHub Copilot — often included in existing licenses.

### Procedure

**1. Map existing licenses**

| Category | Examples | What it does |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Part of M365** | Microsoft Copilot, Copilot in Teams/Word/Excel/PowerPoint | AI assistant in the entire Microsoft ecosystem |
| **Part of Google** | Gemini in Docs/Sheets/Meet | AI Assistant in Google Workspace |
| **Developer** | GitHub Copilot, Cursor | AI assistants for code and automation |
| **Specialized** | Jasper (Marketing), Synthesia (Video), Notion AI | Specialized tools for specific tasks |

Find out: What do we have in licenses? What of it is actually used? Who has access?

**2. Identify the gaps**

For each team/department, ask:
- What recurring tasks does the team tackle?
- Where do they spend most of their time doing routine work?
- What tools would they need?

**3. Find quick wins**

Look for situations where:
- The AI tool is available, but no one is using it
- A simple change (settings, training) can bring big savings
- One person is already using AI successfully and can inspire others

💡 **Tip:** Start from the department where there is the greatest willingness to experiment. Success there motivates others.

**4. Create an overview**

Write the findings in a simple document:
- **We have and use:** (tool → who → for what)
- **We have and we don't use:** (tool → reason → action)
- **We don't have and we need:** (need → recommended tool → benefit estimate)

### Output

- [ ] Complete map of AI tools in the company
- [ ] Identified gaps and unused licenses
- [ ] List of quick wins (quick opportunities)
- [ ] Draft priorities for AI adoption

### 💭 Reflection

- What surprised you while mapping?
- Where do you see the biggest opportunity?

## Exercise 2: Start AI adoption in the team

> **💡 Drive AI adoption across your team — systematically, measurably, and without resistance.**

**⏱️ 45-60 minutes · Level: Manager**

**Goal:** Create a concrete plan for introducing AI tools into your team's work.

### What will you do?You create an AI adoption action plan with three horizons: quick wins (this week), mid-term projects (this month), and strategic initiatives (this quarter).

### Why is it important?

Most AI initiatives fail not on technology but on adoption. People are afraid of change, don't have time to experiment, or don't see the benefit. A systematic approach overcomes these barriers.

### Procedure

**1. Identify the barriers**

Typical barriers and how to overcome them:

| Barrier | Solution |
|---------|--------|
| "I don't have time" | Start with 15 minute tasks, show concrete saving |
| "This is too complicated" | Prepare ready-made prompts and templates |
| "What if I make a mistake?" | Create a safe environment for experiments |
| "This will replace my job" | Show that AI augments capabilities, not replaces humans |
| "I do not have access" | Provide licenses and accesses (see Exercise 1) |

**2. Create 3 Horizons**

**Horizon 1 — This Week (quick wins)**
- Organize a 30-minute workshop "AI in 30 minutes"
- Prepare 5 ready-made prompts for your team's typical tasks
- Show a concrete example of savings (email, summary, analysis)

**Horizon 2 — This Month (Projects)**
- Select 2-3 recurring tasks to automate
- Create shared AI assistants for the team (GPTs, Projects, Gems — see Workbook 1, Exercise 7)
- Set regular experience sharing (e.g. 15 minutes at a team meeting)

**Horizon 3 — This Quarter (Strategy)**
- Create AI guidelines for the team (yes, no, security)
- Identify and train **AI Champions** in the team (people who AI pull and help others)
- Measure savings and benefits
- Share results with management

**3. Prepare specific materials**

For Horizon 1, prepare:
- **5 prompts for your team** — customize your department's task types
- **1 page guide** — how to get started with AI (registration, bookmarks, first task)
- **FAQ** — answers to typical concerns

💡 **Tip:** Let the team choose their own pace. Not everyone has to be on the same level. The important thing is that everyone takes a step forward.

⚠️ **SAFETY:** Adoption must include clear rules:
- What can/can't be entered into public AI tools
- Which tools are approved for corporate data
- How to handle AI outputs (always check!)

### Output

- [ ] Identified barriers to adoption in the team
- [ ] 3 horizons with specific actions
- [ ] Prepared materials for Horizon 1
- [ ] Clear safety rules

### 💭 Reflection

- What barriers are the strongest in your team?
- Who could be your "AI champion"?
- How will you measure success?

## Exercise 3: Create an app or automation for the team

> **💡 Create a practical tool that makes work easier for the whole team — without programming.**

**⏱️ 45-60 minutes · Level: Intermediate/Advanced**

**Goal:** Create a simple application or automation that solves a specific problem for your team.

### What will you do?

You identify a recurring problem, choose a suitable tool and create a functional solution — a calculator, dashboard, chatbot, or automation.

### Why is it important?

- Automation saves time for the whole team, not just one person
- A simple tool can replace hours of manual work
- Shows the concrete value of AI to the entire organization

### Procedure

**1. Identify the problem**

Look for tasks that:
- Repeat every day/week
- They are time consuming but routine
- They require the processing of data or documents

**Examples:**
- Calculator for quotes
- Dashboard for measuring KPIs
- FAQ chatbot for customers
- Automatic report generator
- Form with AI processing

**2. Select a tool**

| Tool | What can | For whom |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **Replit** (https://replit.com) | Web Apps, Calculators, Dashboards | Rapid Prototyping |
| **Lovable** (https://lovable.dev) | Similar to Replit, different interface | Visual creation |
| **Cursor** (https://cursor.sh) | More complex application, file automation | Advanced Projects |
| **ChatGPT GPTs** | Chatbot with own data | FAQ, consultation |
| **Microsoft Power Automate** | Automation in the M365 ecosystem | Company processes |**3. Create the first version**

For example in Replit:
1. Open https://replit.com → Create Repl → HTML, CSS, JS
2. Tell the AI what you want: *"Create me a web calculator for price offers. The user selects products from the offer, enters the quantity and the calculator calculates the price with VAT and discount."*
3. Iterate: test → modify → test again

💡 **Tip:** Start simple. The first version may not be perfect. The important thing is that it works and solves the problem.

**4. Test with the team**

- Give the tool to 2-3 colleagues to try out
- Collect feedback
- Adjust as needed

⚠️ **SECURITY:**
- Consult with IT for sensitive data and business processes
- Start with non-critical tools and public data
- If the tool works with company data, ensure adequate security

### Output

- [ ] Identified issue for automation
- [ ] Appropriate tool selected
- [ ] Functional first version of application/automation
- [ ] Feedback from colleagues

### 💭 Reflection

- How much time per week can the tool save?
- What other problems would you like to automate?

## Exercise 4: Working with AI agents

> **💡 Use specialized AI agents for management tasks — from project planning to knowledge extraction.**

**⏱️ 30-45 minutes · Level: Advanced**

**Goal:** To test and deploy specialized AI agents for specific management tasks.

### What will you do?

You will meet specialized AI agents, choose one for your current problem and try it in practice.

### Why is it important?

General purpose AI (ChatGPT, Claude) is great for everything, but specialized agents are significantly better at specific tasks. They have predefined procedures, knowledge and output formats.

### Overview of specialized agents

| Agent | What is | When to use |
|-------|----------|------------|
| **Context Builder** | Creating contextual documents for AI | When you need to prepare materials for an AI assistant |
| **Knowledge Extractor** | Knowledge Extraction from Documents | Analysis of a large number of documents |
| **Product Architect** | Product and Systems Architecture | Design of a new product or system |
| **Prompt Architect** | Creating advanced prompts | Creating complex AI assignments |
| **Research to Prompt** | Research → prompt | Turning research into actionable AI assignments |
| **Expert Panel** | Expert panel for discussion | You need a view from different perspectives |
| **Work Breakdown Structure** | Decomposing projects into tasks | Project planning, estimation |

### Procedure

**1. Choose an agent for your current issue**

Think about it:
- Are you planning a new project? → **Work Breakdown Structure**
- Do you need to analyze documents? → **Knowledge Extractor**
- Are you designing a new product? → **Product Architect**
- Need different views? → **Expert Panel**

**2. Prepare the context**

The quality of the output depends on the quality of the input:
- Describe the problem as specifically as possible
- Attach relevant documents
- Define the expected output

**3. Try an agent**

**Example with Work Breakdown Structure:**```
I need to break this project into tasks:
- Project: Rolling out AI tools in the marketing department (10 people)
- Deadline: 3 months
- Budget: 50,000 CZK for tools
- Goal: Every team member uses AI actively at least once a day

Create a WBS with:
- Main phases
- Concrete tasks and subtasks
- Time estimates
- Dependencies
- Milestones
```**Example with Expert Panel:**```
I need a discussion on: Should we invest in our own AI stack
or use SaaS tools?

Context: Mid-size company, 200 employees, IT team of 8,
budget 500k CZK per year for AI.

I want perspectives from: CTO, CFO, CISO, Head of HR, Line Manager.
```💡 **Tip:** You can use agents in ChatGPT (as GPTs), in Claude (as Projects), or simply insert their instructions into a new chat.

### Output

- [ ] Selected agent for a specific management task
- [ ] Prepared context and input data
- [ ] A proven agent on a real problem
- [ ] Usable output from the agent

### 💭 Reflection

- Which agent do you find most useful?
- How would you integrate agents into the team's work?

## Exercise 5: Automate repetitive processes

> **💡 Automate routine processes and save your entire team dozens of hours per month.**

**⏱️ 45-60 minutes · Level: Advanced**

**Goal:** Identify and design complex automation that connects multiple steps and tools.

### What will you do?

You will map the process, design the automation, implement it and test it.

### When is automation appropriate?

Automation pays off when:
- The process is repeated min. 1x per week
- Contains 3+ steps
- Steps are predictable (clear rules)
- The process takes 30+ minutes when done manually

### Procedure

**1. Map the process**

Choose a repetitive process and write down each step:

**Example — Monthly reporting:**
1. Download data from CRM
2. Export data from accounting
3. Join both tables
4. Calculate KPIs (turnover, margin, conversions)
5. Create a chart and tables
6. Write a comment on the results
7. Insert the PowerPoint template
8. Email management

**2. Identify what can be automated**

For each step, evaluate:
- Can it be automated? (data download, calculations — yes; strategic commentary — rather no)
- What tool to use for this?

| Task Type | Tool/Access |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Data Download** | API, Power Automate, Scripts |
| **Table Processing** | ChatGPT Code Interpreter, Python, Excel macros |
| **Generating Text** | ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot |
| **Presentation** | Gamma, Beautiful.ai, Copilot in PowerPoint |
| **Shipping** | Power Automate, Gmail automation |
| **Full Workflow** | Make.com, Zapier, n8n |

**3. Start with one step**

Don't automate everything at once. Choose one step and automate it:

**Example — Automatic report:**
Upload the raw data to ChatGPT (Code Interpreter) and request:```
Analyze this data and produce:
1. A table of key KPIs (revenue, MoM growth, number of customers)
2. Charts: revenue by month, top 5 products
3. A short commentary for each KPI (2–3 sentences)
Format: HTML report I can paste into a slide deck.
```**4. Gradually expand**

Once one step works, add another. Gradually connect the steps to the workflow.

💡 **Tip:** Document each automated step — who created it, how it works, what to do if it fails.

⚠️ **SECURITY:**
- Automation with company data must be approved by IT
- Always check AI outputs before sending
- Have a backup manual procedure in case of an outage

### Output

- [ ] Mapped step-by-step process
- [ ] Identified steps for automation
- [ ] Automated at least one step
- [ ] Procedure documentation

### 💭 Reflection

- How much time does automation save?
- What other processes would you like to automate?

## Exercise 6: Assess progress and plan

> **💡 Measure what AI has brought and plan the next steps — the data will convince even the biggest skeptics.**

**⏱️ 30-45 minutes · Level: Manager**

**Goal:** To evaluate AI adoption to date, measure benefits and create a plan for further development.

### What will you do?

You review the results of all previous exercises, quantify the benefits and create a plan for the next quarter.

### Procedure

**1. Rate Team Adoption**

- How many people on the team are actively using AI?
- What tools have taken over? What not?
- What barriers still exist?
- Where are the biggest successes?

**2. Quantify the benefits**

Try to guess:
- How many hours per week does AI save individuals?
- How many hours does it save the whole team?
- Has the quality of the outputs improved? (less mistakes, faster delivery...)
- What is the return on investment? (license vs time saved)

💡 **Tip:** Even a rough estimate is better than none. For example: *"Meeting summary in Teams Copilot saves 15 minutes per meeting × 8 meetings per week × 10 people = 20 hours/week."*

**3. Collect success stories**

Concrete stories are stronger than numbers:
- Who has solved a problem that would otherwise take hours thanks to AI?
- What tool or procedure did the team appreciate the most?
- What was the biggest "aha" moment?

**4. Create a plan for the next quarter**

Based on experience, define:

**What Works — Continued:**
- Tools and procedures that have proven themselves
- Sharing experiences that motivate

**What doesn't work — edit:**
- Tools that didn't take off (why? an alternative?)
- Processes that are too complex (simplify?)

**What's missing — add:**
- New tools or licenses
- Training for advanced technicians
- Automation for other processes

**5. Present the results**

Prepare a short presentation (5 minutes) for management:
- What we tried
- What it brought (figures + stories)
- What are we planning next?
- What we need (license, time, support)

### Output

- [ ] Overview of adoption in the team (who, what, how)
- [ ] Quantified benefits (hours, quality, ROI)
- [ ] Success stories
- [ ] Plan for the next quarter
- [ ] Presentation for management

### 💭 Reflection

- What surprised you during the evaluation?
- What is your strongest argument for expanding AI?
- Where do you see the greatest potential for the next quarter?
