# The Future of No Work — Workbook
## For everyone · From theory to practice

---

## 📖 About this workbook

This workbook is a practical companion to **The Future of No Work** by Filip Dřímalka. The book gives you the ideas and mental models for working with AI; this workbook walks you through hands-on exercises so you can:

- ✅ Acquire practical skills of working with AI tools
- ✅ Try different approaches on real tasks
- ✅ Gradually develop your AI competencies
- ✅ Apply knowledge from the book in practice

### 🎯 How to work with the notebook

1. **Proceed systematically** — the exercises are organized from basic to advanced
2. **Work at your own pace** — each exercise has an estimated time, but don't rush
3. **Experiment** — don't be afraid to try different approaches and tools
4. **Reflect** — note what you learned after each exercise
5. **Share** — discuss with colleagues and share experiences

### 📋 Exercise overview

1. **Set up quick access to AI** — All tools at one click
2. **Create AI Prompt Generator** — Assistant for creating better prompts
3. **Find an AI Tool for Your Needs** — Discover a tool that will save you hours
4. **Compare Two AI Tools** — Find out which one works better for your job
5. **Working with data and documents** — When is chat enough and when do you need more
6. **Build a second brain (3C Framework)** — A system where important information does not get lost
7. **Create your own AI Assistant** — AI that knows your work and style
8. **Your personal AI setup** — A complete system tailored to your work

## Exercise 1: Set up quick access to AI tools

> **💡 Have all AI tools at your fingertips — save minutes every day.**

**⏱️ 15-20 minutes · Level: Basic**

**Goal:** Have all the important AI tools and materials at your fingertips. Register, set bookmarks and try the first tasks.

### What will you do?

You'll sign up for four main AI tools, create an organized bookmark bar, and try your first hands-on tasks.

### Procedure

**1. Show the bookmarks bar in the browser**

- **Chrome/Edge:** `Ctrl + Shift + B` (Windows) or `Cmd + Shift + B` (Mac)
- **Firefox:** Right click on the top bar → Check "Bookmarks bar"
- **Safari:** View → Show favorites bar

**2. Sign up for AI apps and add them to your bar**

| Tool | What is it best for | Link |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| **ChatGPT** | Universal Assistant — Writing, Brainstorming, Explaining | https://chat.openai.com |
| **Claude** | Longer texts, detailed analyses, work with documents | https://claude.ai |
| **Gemini** | Google integration, current information from the site | https://gemini.google.com |
| **NotebookLM** | Analysis of PDF files and documents | https://notebooklm.google.com |

⚠️ **SECURITY:**
- Use a personal email (Gmail, Outlook, etc.), not your work email
- NEVER enter sensitive company data into public AI tools
- Use approved tools for company data (Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI)

**3. Create a folder for materials**

Right-click on the bookmarks bar → "Add folder" → name it "📁 AI Materials". Save links to materials, tips, and other resources in your folder.

**4. Try the first tasks** (10 minutes)

Open ChatGPT or Claude and try:

- **Email:** *"Write a professional email to my colleague that I cannot attend tomorrow's meeting. The email should be polite and brief."*
- **Summary:** Find an article, copy the text to the AI and ask: *"Summarize it for me in 5 key points."*
- **Presentation:** *"Give me the structure of a 10-minute presentation on [your topic]. Introduction, 3 main points, conclusion."*

### Output

- [ ] Four AI tools registered and bookmarked
- [ ] Materials folder created
- [ ] Attempted at least one practical task

### 💭 Reflection

- Which tool interested you the most?
- Which tool do you plan to use first?
- What surprised you about the quality of the outputs?

## Exercise 2: Create an AI Prompt Generator

> **💡 Create an assistant that will make you do much better prompts.**

**⏱️ 15-20 minutes · Level: Basic**

**Goal:** Have your own "assignment generator" that will create professional prompts for any AI tool.

### What will you do?

You create a specialized AI assistant (or save a template) that will create a complete assignment for the AI tools for you. Instead of thinking about the formulation, you leave this work to the generator.

### Why is it important?

- Input quality = output quality
- You don't have to think about the structure, you just describe what you need
- Consistent quality of all your AI submissions
- Works in any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini)

### Procedure

**1. Get Prompt**

You can use the **ready-made Elite Prompt Engineer** (download from https://drimalka.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Elite-Prompt-Engineer_CZ.md — Czech; ask your AI to translate the instructions if you need English) — or copy this basic prompt:

```
You are my AI Prompt Generator. Your job is to write professional, complete prompts for AI assistants and agents.

When I tell you what I need, do this:

1. FIRST ask for the key details:
   - What is the context and goal?
   - Who is the audience or end user?
   - What output format do we need?
   - Any specific requirements?

2. THEN produce a complete prompt that includes:
   - A clear role and context
   - Concrete tasks and responsibilities
   - Expected style and tone
   - Output format
   - Examples (if helpful)

3. EXPLAIN how to use the prompt:
   - Where to run it (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini…)
   - How to tweak it
   - How to turn it into an assistant (GPT, Project, Gem, Agent)

Your outputs should be professional, detailed, and ready to paste.

Do you understand your role? Can we start?
```**2. Save your prompt — choose a method**

| The way | For whom | How to |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Text in Notes** | The easiest start | Paste into OneNote / Notion / Google Keep. When using copy → paste into a new chat. |
| **Specialized Assistant** | Frequent use | **ChatGPT:** My GPTs → Create a GPT. **Claude:** Projects → New Project. **Gemini:** Gems → New Gem. **Copilot:** Create Agent. Always put a prompt in instructions. |
| **Hotkey** | Power users | **Mac:** Settings → Keyboard → Text Shortcuts → `;;prompt`. **Windows:** PowerToys → Text Expander. |

**3. Test**

Enter your Prompt Generator:```
Create a prompt for an AI assistant that will analyze meeting transcripts.
```The Prompt Generator should: ask for details → create a complete prompt → explain how to use it.

### Output

- [ ] AI Prompt Generator saved (in notes / as an assistant / as a shortcut)
- [ ] Tried on one test prompt
- [ ] Functional — creates usable inputs for the AI

### 💭 Reflection

- Which storage method suits you best?
- What type of prompts will you need most often?

## Exercise 3: Find an AI tool for your needs

> **💡 Discover a tool that saves you hours of work per week — ask AI instead of Google.**

**⏱️ 10-15 minutes · Level: Basic**

**Goal:** Find and test an AI tool that solves your specific work problem.

### What will you do?

You identify a specific problem from your work, ask the AI for recommendations, and try out the recommended tool.

### Procedure

**1. Identify the problem**

What's holding you back the most at work? Write it as specifically as possible:

**Examples:**
- "I need to quickly edit videos for Instagram"
- "I have to write a lot of similar emails"
- "I want to automatically generate meeting notes"
- "I need to analyze Excel data"
- "I'm looking for a presentation tool"

💡 **Tip:** The more specific you are, the better recommendations you will get from the AI!

**2. Ask AI for recommendations**

Open ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot or Perplexity and use a prompt like:

```
I need a tool to edit video for social media.
I want to:
- Automatically split a long video into shorter clips
- Add captions
- Optimize for Instagram and TikTok

Which AI tool do you recommend? Give me the top 3 options
with pricing and main features.
```

Other example prompts:

```
Every week I write similar offer emails to customers.
It takes 5+ hours. Is there an AI tool that could help?
I prefer something with a free tier or affordable pricing.
```

```
I heard about Gamma, Beautiful.ai, and Tome for presentations.
Which is best for [your use case]? Compare them on
price, features, and ease of use.
```

The AI will usually suggest 2–5 tools with a short description, pricing, and who each tool fits best.

**3. Try the tool** (5-10 minutes)

1. Open the website of the recommended tool → Sign up / Get started
2. Try on a **specific task** from your job (not in general!)
3. Evaluate: Is a paid plan worth it? Could the whole team benefit? How often will you use it?

⚠️ **Safety:** Use public examples and sample data for test tools. Do not put sensitive company data in them.

### Output

- [ ] Specific problem identified
- [ ] List of recommended tools from AI
- [ ] Tried at least one tool on a real task

### 💭 Reflection

- Which tool suited you best and why?
- What other problem would you like to solve?

## Exercise 4: Compare two AI tools

> **💡 Find out which AI tool is right for you — with a concrete example from your work.**

**⏱️ 20-30 minutes · Level: Basic**

**Goal:** To test two AI tools with the same assignment and see which one works better for your type of work.

### What will you do?

You choose a work task, prepare a prompt and enter it into two different AI tools. You compare the results and find out when to use which tool.

### Procedure

**1. Select a task from your job**

Choose one type of task that you really need to solve:

- **Content creation** — AI helps you write texts, emails, presentations
- **Finding a solution** — you need to find a solution to a problem, choose a system, plan a process
- **Strategic Discussion** — "What would you do in my situation if you wanted to solve/achieve xyz..."

💡 **Tip:** Choose a task that you really need to solve — you will get experience and a useful result.

**2. Prepare prompt**

Write the input that you will use in both tools. You can create one using the AI ​​Prompt Generator from Exercise 2, or write your own — be specific, include the context, and what you expect.

**Important:** Use the **exact same prompt** in both tools.

**3. Test in two tools**

| Tool | Specialty |
|---------|-----------|
| **ChatGPT** | Universal, fast, structured |
| **Claude** | Better for long texts, analyses, complex problems |
| **Gemini** | Access to the site, integration with Google |
| **Microsoft Copilot** | For users of the Microsoft ecosystem |

Open two different tools, put the same prompt and watch:

| Criterion | What to watch |
|-----------|-------------|
| ⏱️ Speed ​​| How fast will you get a response? |
| 📝 Structure | Is the output clear? |
| 🎯 Relevance | Does it exactly match your requirement? |
| 💡 Usefulness | Can you use it straight away? |
| 🧠 Depth | How detailed does the tool elaborate on the topic? |

💡 **Tip:** Take a screenshot or copy the answers to compare.

**4. Rate it**

- Which output was of better quality and required less editing?
- Which would save you more time?
- For this type of task — which tool will you use next?

💡 **Trust your intuition** — it's your job and you know best what works for you.

### Output

- [ ] Same task tested in two AI tools
- [ ] Comparison of results
- [ ] Deciding which tool to use for what

### 💭 Reflection

- What types of tasks would you entrust to which tool?
- What surprised you about the differences?

## Exercise 5: Working with data and documents

> **💡 Learn to decide when chat is enough and when you need advanced tools — save time and nerves.**

**⏱️ 20-30 minutes · Level: Medium**

**Goal:** Understand the differences between simple chat and advanced tools for working with data and documents. Try both approaches.

### What will you do?

You'll walk through a decision matrix, test chat on a simple task, and identify where you need advanced tools in your work.

### When is chat enough vs. when you need more

| | **Chat** (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) | **Advanced Tools** (Cursor, Scripts, Automation) |
|---|---|---|
| **Data** | 1-2 small tables (up to ~1000 rows), one-time analysis, clean data, basic operations | 5+ Tables, Clean and Transform, Repeat Regularly, Thousands+ Rows || **Documents** | 1-2 short documents (up to ~50 pages), single summary, PDF/Word/TXT | Dozens-hundreds of documents, various formats, searching the entire database, tracking changes |

### Procedure

**1. Try the chat on a simple task**

- **With data:** Find a small table (up to 100 rows), upload to ChatGPT/Claude and ask: *"Analyze this data and tell me 3 key findings."*
- **With Document:** Find a short document (PDF or text), upload to AI and ask: *"Summarize this document into 5 key points."*

**2. Identify Advanced Needs**

Think about your work:
- Do you have repetitive tasks with data or documents?
- Do you work with a large number of files?
- Do you need to automate processing?

If so — there are tools that can handle it. **Cursor** is not only for programmers: it can convert formats (PDF→Markdown, CSV→JSON, Excel→PDF), download data from the web, organize files, and all this based on commands in Czech.

**Other advanced tools:**
- **Replit** (https://replit.com) — rapid application prototyping, calculators, dashboards
- **Lovable** (https://lovable.dev) — similar functionality as Replit, different interface
- **Cursor** (https://cursor.sh) — AI agent for knowledge work and automation
- **GitHub Copilot** (https://github.com/features/copilot) — AI assistant in the code editor

💡 **Corporate tip:** GitHub Copilot is often included in the corporate license. Ask IT.

⚠️ **SECURITY:** These tools are for experimentation and learning. Use trial or public information for testing. Solve sensitive company data in company AI tools.

### Output

- [ ] Tested chat on a simple task (data or document)
- [ ] Understanding the differences between chat and advanced tools
- [ ] Identified cases where you need advanced tools

### 💭 Reflection

- Which approach suits you better?
- What tasks would you like to automate?

## Exercise 6: Build your second brain (3C Framework)

> **💡 Build a system where no information falls by the wayside — capture, process, connect.**

**⏱️ 45-60 minutes · Level: Medium**

**Goal:** Set up a complete system for working with information according to the 3C Framework: **Capture** (capture) → **Clean** (process) → **Connect** (connect).

### What will you do?

You will go through the three pillars of working with information, choose appropriate tools and set up a working system.

---

### Step 1: CAPTURE — Capturing information

Goal: Have a system where you can quickly store anything important.

**Tools for quick tasks (to-do):**
- **Apple Reminders** — for the Apple ecosystem
- **Google Tasks** — integration with Gmail and Google Calendar
- **Microsoft To Do** — integration with Outlook and M365

**Notepads:**
- **Evernote** — the pioneer of digital notes
- **OneNote** — Microsoft notepad
- **Notion** — all-in-one workspace
- **Obsidian** — local note system

**Meeting Transcripts:**
- **Microsoft Teams Copilot** — integrated in Teams
- **TLDV** — for Google Meet and Zoom
- **Fireflies.ai** — AI assistant for transcribing meetings

**Web scraping and data extraction (advanced):**
- **Firecrawl** — Web crawling API
- **Exa** — AI-powered search engine
- **Apify** — web scraping platform

**Your task:**
1. Choose one to-do tool and one notebook
2. Install on both phone and computer
3. Save the first 3 things (link, note, task)
4. Create a basic folder structure:```
📁 Projects
  📁 Project A
  📁 Project B
📁 Reference
  📁 Articles
  📁 Tools
📁 Personal
  📁 Ideas
  📁 Learning
```---

### Step 2: CLEAN — Processing information

Goal: Learn how to efficiently process stored information using AI.

**Format conversion:**
- PDF → Markdown: Upload PDF to Claude/ChatGPT, request conversion
- Excel → CSV: Use Cursor or ChatGPT Code Interpreter

**Processing Appointments:**
Upload the meeting transcript to AI and request:
*"Create from this transcript: summary of main points, list of action points with assigned people, key decisions."*

**Creating Templates:**
1. Find 3-5 similar documents (emails, reports)
2. Upload to AI
3. Request: *"Create a template based on these documents that captures their structure and style."*

**File organization (advanced):**
In Cursor you can: *"Organize these files into folders by type and date"* — on a copy of the data first!

**Your task:**
1. Try to convert at least one format
2. Process the meeting transcript (if available)
3. Create a template from existing documents

---

### Step 3: CONNECT — Connecting information

Goal: Link stored information using AI assistants and knowledge bases.

**Create an AI assistant with your documents:**

| Platform | How to |
|-----------|-----------|
| **ChatGPT GPTs** | Explore GPTs → Create → in "Knowledge" upload documents → in "Instructions" describe how it should work |
| **Claude Projects** | Projects → New Project → add documents → start a conversation |
| **NotebookLM** | Upload documents → ask about content |

**Your task:**
1. Create an assistant/project with 3-5 of your documents
2. Ask for specific information from the documents
3. Check that the answers correspond to the content
4. Optional: create an FAQ chatbot — collect FAQs and answers, create a GPT/Project/Gem from them

### Output of the entire exercise

- [ ] Set system for capturing information (to-do + notes)
- [ ] Tried processing (format conversion or template)
- [ ] Created AI assistant with your documents
- [ ] Basic structure of information organization

### 💭 Reflection

- Which tools suit you best?
- What type of information do you store most often?
- How would you like to expand the system?

## Exercise 7: Create your own AI assistant

> **💡 Create an AI that knows your work, your style, and your needs — and use it every day.**

**⏱️ 30-45 minutes · Level: Medium**

**Goal:** Create a specialized AI assistant that solves a specific task from your work.

### What will you do?

You create your own AI assistant in one of the main tools. The assistant will be focused on a specific task — from analyzing reports to writing emails to brainstorming.

### Why is it important?

- **Specialization** — the assistant is focused on your specific task
- **Consistency** — same style and approach every time
- **Time saving** — you don't have to repeat the instructions
- **Sharing** — you can share the assistant with the team

### Procedure

**1. Choose a platform**

| Platform | Advantage | How to get started |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **ChatGPT GPTs** | The easiest start, GPT Store for sharing | My GPTs → Create a GPT → put the instructions in Instructions |
| **Claude Projects** | Great for working with documents, large context (200k tokens), Artifacts | Projects → New Project → Custom Instructions + Documents |
| **Gemini Gems** | Integration with Google Workspace | Gems → New Gem → insert instructions |
| **Microsoft Copilot Agents** | Integration with M365 | Create Agent → insert instructions |

**2. Define the purpose of the assistant**

Think about it:
- What specific task does it have to solve? (analysis of reports, writing emails, brainstorming, code review...)
- What style of communication do you prefer?
- What information does he need to know? (you can upload documents)

**3. Build and Test**

1. Open the selected platform
2. Create a new assistant with instructions describing its role
3. Upload relevant documents (if needed)
4. Try it on several tasks
5. Modify the instructions until you are satisfied

💡 **Tip:** Iterate — the first version won't be perfect. Gradually refine the instructions based on how the assistant responds.

### Output

- [ ] Created custom AI assistant on one of the platforms
- [ ] Tested on real tasks
- [ ] Tailored to your needs

### 💭 Reflection

- What type of assistant suits you best?
- What other assistants would you like to create?

## Exercise 8: Your personal AI setup

> **💡 Build a complete AI setup tailored to your work — and be clear when to use what.**

**⏱️ 30-45 minutes · Level: All**

**Goal:** Create a complete personal system of AI tools and assistants for your work. Combine everything from the previous exercises.

### What will you do?

You will map out your work needs, compile a list of tools, link them into a functional workflow and create documentation of your setup.

### Procedure

**1. Map the job needs**

- What types of tasks do you do most often?
- Where do you waste the most time?
- What information do you need to store and process?
- Who do you work with and how do you communicate?

**2. Build a list of tools**

Based on the experience of the previous exercises, make a list:
- Basic AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — which ones do you use?)
- Specialized tools for your tasks
- Information storage tools (from Exercise 6)
- Custom assistants (from Exercise 7)

**3. Set up a workflow**

Define clear rules:
- When to use which tool?
- How do you organize stored information?
- How do you automate repetitive tasks?

**4. Reflect and Plan**

Go through all the exercises:
- Which ones have you finished? What did you learn?
- What are you good at? What do you want to improve?
- What tools do you want to explore more?
- What automations do you want to create?

Create a plan for the next 3 months — specific steps, what you want to try.

**5. Share experiences**

Discuss what you have learned with your colleagues. Inspire each other.

### Output

- [ ] Complete list of your AI tools and their purpose
- [ ] Set workflow — when to use what
- [ ] Plan for further development
- [ ] Shared experiences with others

### 💭 Reflection

- What is your ideal AI setup?
- What are you missing?
- How do you plan to expand the system?
