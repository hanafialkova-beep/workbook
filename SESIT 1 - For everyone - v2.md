## 📖 About This Workbook

This workbook is a practical companion to the book **"The Future of No Work" (Budoucnost nepráce)** by Filip Dřímalka. While the book provides the theoretical foundations and frameworks, this workbook walks you through concrete exercises — from your first AI experiment to setting up your own AI assistant.

✅ Develop practical skills for working with AI tools
✅ Try different approaches on real work tasks
✅ Gradually build your AI competencies
✅ Apply insights from the book in practice

### 🎯 How to Use This Workbook

1. **Start with Exercise 0** — experience AI hands-on before you start setting anything up
2. **Work at your own pace** — each exercise has an estimated time, but don't rush
3. **Experiment** — don't be afraid to try different approaches and tools
4. **Save what works** — prompts, templates, rules. By the end, you'll build your personal AI toolkit
5. **Share** — discuss with colleagues and share your experiences

### 🔒 Golden Safety Rule

> For the experiments in this workbook, use publicly available data and a personal email account (Gmail, etc.). Sensitive company data belongs only in tools approved by your organization (Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI, etc.). This rule applies to all exercises — we won't repeat it.

## Exercise 0: Solve Your First Problem with AI

> **💡 Before setting anything up, experience what AI can do for you — on a problem you're actually dealing with today.**

**⏱️ 5–10 minutes · Level: Beginner**

### What You'll Do

Open any AI tool and solve a problem you have on your desk right now. You don't need to write a "perfect prompt" — just describe what you need, like you would to a colleague.

### Steps

**1. Open an AI tool**

Go to https://chat.openai.com (works with a Google account) or https://claude.ai. If you already have an account with another tool, use that.

**2. Write to the AI about the problem you're working on**

Any problem. You don't need to think about format — just describe the situation:

**Examples to get you started:**
- *"Help me write a reply to this client email: [paste the email]"*
- *"I have a presentation in 2 hours on topic X. Suggest a structure."*
- *"I need to summarize this article in 3 sentences: [paste the text]"*
- *"What's the best way to politely but clearly decline a collaboration offer?"*
- *"Prepare an agenda for a meeting on [your topic], 30 minutes, 5 participants."*
- *"Write a professional email to a colleague saying I can't attend tomorrow's meeting. Keep it polite and brief."*

**3. Read the response — and iterate**

This is the key skill: if the response didn't help 100%, **tell the AI what's missing or what you'd like changed**. This "back-and-forth" is the foundation of working with AI. Don't hesitate to say:

- *"Make it shorter."*
- *"The tone is too formal, make it more friendly."*
- *"Add some concrete numbers."*
- *"That's not quite what I meant. What I actually need is..."*

## Exercise 2: Create Your AI Prompt Generator

> **💡 Build an assistant that helps you write far better prompts — and learn the key skill: iteration.**

**⏱️ 15–20 minutes · Level: Beginner**

**Goal:** Have your own "prompt generator" that creates professional AI prompts for any task.

### What You'll Do

You'll create a specialized AI assistant (or save a template) that will write complete, high-quality prompts for you. Instead of thinking about how to phrase things, you let the generator handle it.

### Why This Matters

- Quality of prompt = quality of output
- You don't have to think about structure, just describe what you need
- Consistent quality across all your AI prompts
- Works in any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini)

### Steps

**1. Get the prompt**

We recommend downloading the ready-made **Elite Prompt Engineer** from https://drimalka.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Elite-Prompt-Engineer_CZ.md — it's more detailed and battle-tested.

If you prefer a simpler version, copy this basic prompt:

```
You are my AI Prompt Generator. Your role is to create professional,
complete prompts for AI assistants and agents.

When I tell you what I need, you will:

1. FIRST ASK about key details:
   - What is the context and goal?
   - Who is the target audience/user?
   - What output format is needed?
   - What specific requirements exist?

2. THEN CREATE a complete prompt including:
   - A clear role and context
   - Specific tasks and responsibilities
   - Expected style and tone
   - Output format
   - Examples (if relevant)

3. EXPLAIN how to use the prompt:
   - Where to apply it (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini...)
   - How to customize it as needed
   - How to turn it into an assistant (GPT, Project, Gem, Agent)

Your outputs will be professional, detailed, and ready to use.

Understood? Shall we begin?
```

**2. Save your prompt**

Easiest path: paste the prompt into **Claude Projects** (Projects → New Project → Custom Instructions) or **ChatGPT GPTs** (My GPTs → Create a GPT → Instructions). This gives you an assistant you can return to anytime.

**3. Test it**

Give your Prompt Generator this task:

```
Create a prompt for an AI assistant that analyzes meeting transcripts.
```

The Prompt Generator should: ask for details → create a complete prompt → explain how to use it.

**4. Iterate — improve the output**

Look at the generated prompt and tell the generator what you'd like changed:

- *"Add that all outputs should always be in English."*
- *"I want the assistant to always start with a 3-sentence summary."*
- *"Make it simpler — it should be more concise."*

This "back-and-forth" is the key skill for working with AI (same as in Exercise 0).

## Exercise 3: When AI Lies — Learn to Spot It

> **💡 AI always answers with confidence. Learn to tell when it's right and when it's making things up — this is the most important skill for working with AI.**

**⏱️ 10–15 minutes · Level: Beginner**

### Why This Matters

AI is a tool, not an authority. The key skill is not "knowing how to write a prompt" — it's knowing how to evaluate the output. Without this, you can't trust AI in your work.

### What You'll Do

You'll intentionally test where AI is accurate and where it makes things up. You'll learn to recognize so-called "hallucinations" — situations where AI generates plausible but false text.

### Steps

**1. Ask about something you know (3 min)**

Open ChatGPT or Claude and ask a factual question from your field where YOU KNOW the correct answer:
- *"What are the main responsibilities of [your job title]?"*
- *"What regulations govern [your field]?"*
- *"Who is [well-known person in your industry] and what are they known for?"*

Check the answer. It will probably be good — AI is strong on general knowledge.

**2. Ask about something specific (3 min)**

Now ask about something where you suspect AI will be guessing:
- *"What was [your company]'s revenue in 2024?"*
- *"Who wrote [a lesser-known book in your field]?"*
- *"How many employees does [your company] have exactly?"*

Check the answer. It probably contains errors — but AI delivers them with the **same confidence** as correct information.

**3. Try a deliberately false premise (3 min)**

Give AI something that isn't true and watch whether it corrects you:
- *"Would you agree that the Digital Transformation Act of 2025 requires every company to have a Chief AI Officer?"*
- *"Can you tell me more about the book [made-up title] by [real author]?"*

Most AI models will respond as if it were true. That's called a **hallucination** — AI generates probable text, not truthful text.

**4. Write down your rules (3 min)**

Based on what you found, write down 3 rules for working with AI output. Examples:
- *"I will always verify facts, numbers, and citations from an independent source."*
- *"If AI claims something specific about our company, I'll check it."*
- *"A confident tone from AI doesn't mean the answer is correct."*

- [ ] Tested AI on a question where you know the answer
- [ ] Uncovered at least one hallucination or inaccuracy
- [ ] 📌 **3 personal rules for verifying AI outputs** — save these, you'll always need them

## Exercise 4: Find an AI Tool for Your Needs

> **💡 Discover a tool that will save you hours of work every week — ask AI instead of Googling.**

**⏱️ 10–15 minutes · Level: Beginner**

**Goal:** Find and try an AI tool that solves a specific work problem for you.

### What You'll Do

You'll identify a concrete problem from your work, ask AI for recommendations, and try out the recommended tool.

### Steps

**1. Identify the problem**

What slows you down the most at work? Write it as specifically as possible:

**Examples:**
- "I need to quickly edit videos for social media"
- "I have to write a lot of similar emails"
- "I want to automatically generate meeting notes"
- "I need to analyze spreadsheet data"
- "I'm looking for a presentation tool"

💡 **Tip:** The more specific, the better recommendations you'll get from AI!

**2. Ask AI for recommendations**

Open ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, or Perplexity and use a prompt like:

```
I need a tool for editing videos for social media.
I want to:
- Automatically split long videos into shorter clips
- Add subtitles
- Optimize for Instagram and TikTok

Which AI tool do you recommend? Give me the 3 best options
with pricing and key features.
```

More example prompts:

```
I write similar emails to customers every week with offers.
It takes me 5+ hours. Is there an AI tool that could help?
I prefer something with a free version.
```

```
I've heard about tools like Gamma, Beautiful.ai, and Tome
for creating presentations. Which is best for [your use case]?
Compare them on price, features, and ease of use.
```

AI will usually recommend 2–5 tools with descriptions, pricing, and who they're best for.

⚡ **Remember Exercise 3:** AI may recommend a tool that doesn't exist or has different features. Verify recommendations on the tool's own website.

**3. Try the tool** (5–10 minutes)

1. Open the recommended tool's website → Sign up / Get started
2. Try it on a **specific task** from your work (not generically!)
3. Evaluate: Is it worth paying for premium? Could the whole team benefit? How often will I use this?

## Exercise 5: Compare Two AI Tools

> **💡 Find out which AI tool is right for you — with a concrete example from your work.**

**⏱️ 20–30 minutes · Level: Beginner**

**Goal:** Test two AI tools with the same prompt and find out which works better for your type of work.

### What You'll Do

You'll choose a work task, write a prompt, and give it to two different AI tools. You'll compare results and learn when to use which tool.

### Steps

**1. Choose a task from your work**

Pick one type of task you genuinely need to do:

- **Content creation** — AI helps you write texts, emails, presentations
- **Finding solutions** — you need to find a solution to a problem, choose a system, plan a process
- **Strategic discussion** — "What would you do in my situation if you wanted to solve/achieve xyz…"

💡 **Tip:** Choose a task you actually need to solve — you'll get experience and a useful result.

**2. Write a prompt**

Write the prompt you'll use in both tools. You can create it with the AI Prompt Generator from Exercise 2, or write your own — be specific, include context, and say what you expect.

**Important:** Use the **exact same prompt** in both tools.

**3. Test in two tools**

| Tool | Specialty |
|------|-----------|
| **ChatGPT** | Universal, fast, structured |
| **Claude** | Better for long texts, analyses, complex problems |
| **Gemini** | Web access, Google integration |
| **Microsoft Copilot** | For Microsoft ecosystem users |

Open two different tools, paste the same prompt, and observe:

| Criterion | What to watch |
|-----------|---------------|
| ⏱️ Speed | How quickly do you get a response? |
| 📝 Structure | Is the output clear and organized? |
| 🎯 Relevance | Does it answer your request precisely? |
| 💡 Usefulness | Can you use it directly? |
| 🧠 Depth | How thoroughly does the tool develop the topic? |

💡 **Tip:** Take a screenshot or copy the responses so you can compare them.

**4. Evaluate**

- Which output was higher quality and required less editing?
- Which would save you more time?
- For this type of task — which tool will you use next time?

💡 **Trust your instincts** — it's your work task and you know best what helps you.

## Exercise 6: Working with Data and Documents

> **💡 Learn to decide when chat is enough and when you need more — and set up the advanced tools.**

**⏱️ 30–45 minutes · Level: Intermediate / Advanced**

**Goal:** Try working with data and documents in chat, understand when you need advanced tools, and set them up.

### What You'll Do

First you'll try what a regular chat (ChatGPT, Claude) can handle. Then you'll look at the differences between "when chat is enough and when you need more." Finally, you'll set up advanced tools for more complex tasks.

### Steps

**1. Try chat on your own document (10 min)**

Give it a go:

- **With data:** Find a small table (up to 100 rows), upload it to ChatGPT/Claude and ask: *"Analyze this data and give me 3 key insights."*
- **With a document:** Find a short document (PDF or text), upload it to AI and ask: *"Summarize this document into 5 key points."*

⚡ **Remember Exercise 3:** Verify key findings — AI may misinterpret data.

**2. Understand the differences: when chat, when more**

| | **Chat** (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) | **Advanced tools** (Cursor, scripts, automation) |
|---|---|---|
| **Data** | 1–2 small tables (up to ~1,000 rows), one-off analysis, clean data, basic operations | 5+ tables, cleaning and transformation, regular repetition, thousands+ of rows |
| **Documents** | 1–2 short documents (up to ~50 pages), one-off summarization, PDF/Word/TXT | Dozens–hundreds of documents, various formats, searching across an entire knowledge base, tracking changes |

Think about your work:
- Do you have recurring tasks with data or documents?
- Do you work with large quantities of files?
- Do you need to automate processing?

If yes — continue to step 3.

**3. Set up advanced tools**

**App creation — Replit or Lovable:**
1. Register at **Replit** (https://replit.com) or **Lovable** (https://lovable.dev)
2. Try generating something simple (e.g., a calculator or to-do list)

**Knowledge work and automation — Cursor:**
1. Install **Cursor** (https://cursor.sh) or use **GitHub Copilot**
2. Try the agent: drag a PDF into Cursor and type *"Convert this PDF to markdown format"*
3. You can write commands in plain English!

💡 **Cursor** looks like a code editor, but you don't need to know how to code. Just type commands in natural language.

💡 **Corporate tip:** GitHub Copilot is often included in your company's license. Ask your IT department.

**Optionally:** **Relay.app** or **n8n** for workflow automation — only if you're interested in connecting apps.

## Exercise 8: Create Your Own AI Assistant

> **💡 Build an AI that knows your work, your style, and your needs — and use it every day.**

**⏱️ 20–30 minutes · Level: Intermediate**

**Goal:** Create a specialized AI assistant that handles a specific task from your work.

### What You'll Do

You'll create your own AI assistant on one of the main platforms. The assistant will be focused on a specific task — from analyzing reports to writing emails to brainstorming.

### Why This Matters

- **Specialization** — the assistant is focused on your specific task
- **Consistency** — the same style and approach every time
- **Time saving** — you don't have to repeat instructions
- **Sharing** — you can share the assistant with your team

### Steps

**1. Choose your platform**

We recommend starting with **ChatGPT GPTs** — it's the simplest: My GPTs → Create a GPT → paste your instructions into Instructions.

**3. Create and test**

1. Open your chosen platform
2. Create a new assistant with instructions describing its role
3. Upload relevant documents (if needed)
4. Try it on several tasks

**4. Iterate — improve the instructions**

This is the most important step. The first version won't be perfect. Notice:
- Where does the assistant respond well?
- Where does it respond poorly or vaguely?
- What is it missing?

Adjust the instructions and try again. Repeat until you're satisfied.
