## 📖 About This Workbook

This workbook is a practical companion to the book **"The Future of No Work" (Budoucnost nepráce)** by Filip Dřímalka, focused on managers, team leaders, and everyone who drives AI adoption within their teams and organizations. While Workbook 1 ("For Everyone") focuses on personal productivity, this workbook addresses:

✅ How to map and leverage AI tools in your company
✅ How to systematically introduce AI into your team's work
✅ How to find and support the people who will drive AI adoption
✅ How to create tools and automations for the whole team
✅ How to work with specialized AI agents
✅ How to measure the success of AI adoption

### 🎯 Prerequisites

We recommend completing Workbook 1 ("For Everyone") first, so you have hands-on experience with AI tools. This workbook builds on that and takes it to the team and organizational level.

### 🔒 Golden Safety Rule

> When working with AI tools, always respect your company's data handling policies. Sensitive company data belongs only in tools approved by your organization (Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI, etc.). This rule applies to all exercises — we won't repeat it.

## Exercise 1: Map AI Tools in Your Company

> **💡 Find out which AI tools you already have available and which ones you're missing — you might be surprised.**

**⏱️ 30–45 minutes · Level: Managerial**

**Goal:** Get a complete picture of the AI tools available in your company, identify gaps, and find opportunities.

### What You'll Do

You'll map existing AI tools in your company, identify who uses them (and who doesn't), and find areas where AI can deliver the greatest value.

### Why This Matters

Many companies are already paying for AI tools without fully using them. Microsoft 365 Copilot, Google Workspace AI, GitHub Copilot — they're often included in existing licenses.

### Steps

**1. Map existing licenses**

| Category | Examples | What it does |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Part of M365** | Microsoft Copilot, Copilot in Teams/Word/Excel/PowerPoint | AI assistant across the Microsoft ecosystem |
| **Part of Google** | Gemini in Docs/Sheets/Meet | AI assistant in Google Workspace |
| **Developer tools** | GitHub Copilot, Cursor | AI assistants for code and automation |
| **Specialized** | Jasper (marketing), Synthesia (video), Notion AI | Specialized tools for specific tasks |

Find out: What do we have in our licenses? What's actually being used? Who has access?

**2. Identify gaps**

For each team/department, ask:
- What recurring tasks does the team handle?
- Where do they spend the most time on routine work?
- What tools would they need?

**3. Find quick wins**

Look for situations where:
- An AI tool is available but nobody uses it
- A simple change (setup, training) can deliver big savings
- One person is already successfully using AI and can inspire others

💡 **Tip:** Start with the department most willing to experiment. Success there motivates everyone else.

**4. Create a summary**

Write your findings into a simple document:
- **Have and use:** (tool → who → for what)
- **Have but don't use:** (tool → reason → action)
- **Don't have but need:** (need → recommended tool → estimated benefit)

- [ ] Complete map of AI tools in the company
- [ ] Identified gaps and unused licenses
- [ ] List of quick wins (fast opportunities)
- [ ] Proposed priorities for AI adoption

## Exercise 2: Launch AI Adoption in Your Team

> **💡 Kick off AI adoption in your team — systematically, measurably, and without resistance.**

**⏱️ 45–60 minutes · Level: Managerial**

**Goal:** Create a concrete plan for introducing AI tools into your team's work.

### What You'll Do

You'll create an AI adoption action plan with three horizons: quick wins (this week), medium-term projects (this month), and strategic initiatives (this quarter).

### Why This Matters

Most AI initiatives fail not because of the technology, but because of adoption. People fear change, don't have time to experiment, or don't see the value. A systematic approach overcomes these barriers.

### Steps

**1. Identify barriers**

Typical barriers and how to overcome them:

| Barrier | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| "I don't have time" | Start with 15-minute tasks, show concrete time savings |
| "This is too complicated" | Prepare ready-made prompts and templates |
| "What if I make a mistake?" | Create a safe space for experimentation |
| "This will replace my job" | Show that AI extends capabilities, doesn't replace people |
| "I don't have access" | Secure licenses and access (see Exercise 1) |

**2. Create 3 horizons**

**Horizon 1 — This week (quick wins)**
- Organize a 30-minute workshop "AI in 30 minutes"
- Prepare 5 ready-made prompts for typical tasks in your team
- Show a concrete example of time savings (email, summary, analysis)

**Horizon 2 — This month (projects)**
- Select 2–3 recurring tasks for automation
- Create shared AI assistants for the team (GPTs, Projects, Gems — see Workbook 1, Exercise 8)
- Set up regular experience sharing (e.g., 15 minutes at your team meeting)

**Horizon 3 — This quarter (strategy)**
- Create AI guidelines for the team (what's OK, what's not, security)
- Identify and support AI champions in the team (see Exercise 3)
- Measure savings and benefits
- Share results with management

**3. Prepare concrete materials**

For Horizon 1, prepare:
- **5 prompts for your team** — tailored to your department's typical tasks
- **1-page starter guide** — how to get started with AI (registration, bookmarks, first task)
- **FAQ** — answers to typical concerns

💡 **Tip:** Let the team choose their own pace. Not everyone has to be at the same level. The important thing is for each person to take one step forward.

⚠️ **SAFETY:** Adoption must include clear rules:
- What can and cannot be entered into public AI tools
- Which tools are approved for company data
- How to handle AI outputs (always verify!)

### Your Output

- [ ] Identified adoption barriers in the team
- [ ] 3 horizons with concrete actions
- [ ] Materials prepared for Horizon 1
- [ ] Clear safety rules

## Exercise 3: Find Your AI Champion

> **💡 Every team needs someone to drive AI adoption. You might already have them — you just don't know it yet.**

**⏱️ 15–20 minutes · Level: Managerial**

**Goal:** Identify people in your team who can become drivers of AI adoption and agree on next steps with them.

### What You'll Do

You'll look through your team and identify people who have the potential to become AI champions — the ones who help others and drive adoption from within.

### Why This Matters

AI adoption works best when it's driven by people inside the team — not by top-down mandate. One enthusiastic colleague who shows others how AI saves them time is more effective than any training session.

### Steps

**1. Identify candidates**

Think through your team (in your head or on paper) and answer:

- Who in your team is already trying AI tools on their own?
- Who enjoys sharing tips and tricks with others?
- Who has a natural curiosity and willingness to experiment?
- Who combines domain expertise with technology?

Write down 1–2 names.

**2. Assess their potential**

| Quality | Why it matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **Willingness to experiment** | Not afraid to try a new tool, even if it doesn't work at first |
| **Sharing** | When something works, they tell others about it |
| **Domain expertise** | Understands the team's work and knows where AI can help |
| **Colleagues' trust** | Others listen and respect their recommendations |

💡 **Tip:** An AI champion doesn't have to be an IT specialist. It can be anyone — from an admin to a marketer to a salesperson. The important thing is the willingness to try and share.

**3. Agree on next steps**

For each potential AI champion:
- Schedule a 15-minute 1:1
- Ask what they're already experimenting with in AI
- Ask what they'd need to help others
- Offer support: time, tools, space at team meetings

**4. Define the role**

An AI champion should:
- Help colleagues with their first steps
- Collect and share working prompts and procedures
- Be the contact person for AI questions
- Have dedicated time (e.g., 2 hours per week) for experimenting and preparation

- [ ] 1–2 names of potential AI champions in your team
- [ ] 1:1 scheduled with at least one of them

## Exercise 4: Build an App or Automation for Your Team

> **💡 Create a practical tool that makes work easier for the whole team — no coding required.**

**⏱️ 45–60 minutes · Level: Intermediate/Advanced**

**Goal:** Create a simple application or automation that solves a specific recurring problem for your team.

### What You'll Do

You'll identify a recurring problem, choose the right tool, and create a working solution — a calculator, dashboard, chatbot, or automation.

### Why This Matters

- Automation saves time for the whole team, not just one person
- A simple tool can replace hours of manual work
- It demonstrates concrete AI value to the entire organization

### Steps

**1. Identify the problem**

Look for tasks that:
- Repeat every day or week
- Are time-consuming but routine
- Require data or document processing
- Have 3+ steps and are predictable

**Examples:**
- Calculator for pricing quotes
- Dashboard for measuring KPIs
- FAQ chatbot for customers
- Automated report generator
- Form with AI processing
- Monthly reporting (download data → process → create presentation → send)

**2. Choose your tool**

| Tool | What it does | Best for |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Replit** (https://replit.com) | Web apps, calculators, dashboards | Quick prototypes |
| **Lovable** (https://lovable.dev) | Similar to Replit, different interface | Visual creation |
| **Cursor** (https://cursor.sh) | More complex apps, file automation | Advanced projects |
| **ChatGPT GPTs** | Chatbot with custom data | FAQ, consultations |
| **Microsoft Power Automate** | Automation in the M365 ecosystem | Company processes |
| **Make.com / Zapier / n8n** | Connecting apps into workflows | Full process automation |

**3. Create your first version**

For example in Replit:
1. Go to https://replit.com → Create Repl → HTML, CSS, JS
2. Describe to AI what you want: *"Create a web calculator for pricing quotes. The user selects products from a list, enters quantities, and the calculator computes the price including tax and discount."*
3. Iterate: test → adjust → test again

Or in ChatGPT (for automating a report):
```
Analyze this data and create:
1. A table with key KPIs (revenue, MoM growth, number of customers)
2. Charts: monthly revenue, top 5 products
3. Commentary on each KPI (2–3 sentences)
Format: HTML report that I can embed in a presentation.
```

💡 **Tip:** Start simple. The first version doesn't have to be perfect. The important thing is that it works and solves the problem.

**4. Test with your team**

- Give the tool to 2–3 colleagues to try
- Collect feedback
- Adjust based on their needs

**5. Expand gradually**

Once one step works, add another. Gradually connect the steps into a workflow.

💡 **Tip:** Document every automated step — who created it, how it works, what to do if it fails.

⚠️ **SAFETY:**
- For sensitive data and company processes, consult IT
- Start with non-critical tools and publicly available data
- Automations involving company data must be approved by IT
- Always review AI outputs before sending
- Have a backup manual process in case of failure

- [ ] Selected the right tool

- [ ] Feedback from colleagues

## Exercise 5: Automate Recurring Processes

> **💡 Automate routine processes and save your entire team dozens of hours every month.**

**⏱️ 45–60 minutes · Level: Advanced**

**Goal:** Identify and design a comprehensive automation that connects multiple steps and tools.

### What You'll Do

You'll map a recurring process step by step, decide what can be automated, and implement at least one step.

### When Automation Makes Sense

Automation is worthwhile when:
- The process repeats at least once a week
- It has 3+ steps
- The steps are predictable (clear rules)
- The process takes 30+ minutes when done manually

### Steps

**1. Map the process**

Choose a recurring process and write down every step:

**Example — Monthly reporting:**
1. Download data from CRM
2. Export data from accounting
3. Merge both tables
4. Calculate KPIs (revenue, margin, conversion)
5. Create charts and tables
6. Write commentary on results
7. Insert into PowerPoint template
8. Send by email to management

**2. Identify what can be automated**

For each step, evaluate:
- Can it be automated? (downloading data, calculations — yes; strategic commentary — probably not)
- Which tool to use?

| Task type | Tool/Approach |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Downloading data** | API, Power Automate, scripts |
| **Processing tables** | ChatGPT Code Interpreter, Python, Excel macros |
| **Generating text** | ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot |
| **Presentations** | Gamma, Beautiful.ai, Copilot in PowerPoint |
| **Sending** | Power Automate, Gmail automation |
| **Full workflow** | Make.com, Zapier, n8n |

**3. Start with one step**

Don't automate everything at once. Choose one step and automate it:

**Example — Automated report:**
Upload raw data into ChatGPT (Code Interpreter) and ask:
```
Analyze this data and create:
1. A table with key KPIs (revenue, MoM growth, number of customers)
2. Charts: monthly revenue, top 5 products
3. Commentary on each KPI (2–3 sentences)
Format: HTML report that I can embed in a presentation.
```

**4. Expand gradually**

Once one step works, add the next. Gradually connect the steps into a workflow.

💡 **Tip:** Document every automated step — who created it, how it works, what to do if it fails.

⚠️ **SAFETY:**
- Automations involving company data must be approved by IT
- Always review AI outputs before sending
- Have a backup manual process in case of failure

### Your Output

- [ ] Process mapped step by step
- [ ] Identified steps for automation
- [ ] At least one step automated
- [ ] Documentation of the process

## Exercise 6: Working with AI Agents

> **💡 Use specialized AI agents for managerial tasks — from project planning to knowledge extraction.**

**⏱️ 30–45 minutes · Level: Advanced**

**Goal:** Try and deploy specialized AI agents for specific managerial tasks.

### What You'll Do

You'll learn about specialized AI agents, choose one for your current problem, and try it in practice.

### Why This Matters

General-purpose AI (ChatGPT, Claude) is great for everything, but specialized agents are significantly better for specific tasks. They have predefined workflows, knowledge, and output formats.

### Overview of Specialized Agents

| Agent | For what | When to use |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Context Builder** | Creating context documents for AI | When you need to prepare briefing materials for an AI assistant |
| **Knowledge Extractor** | Extracting knowledge from documents | Analyzing a large number of documents |
| **Product Architect** | Product and system architecture | Designing a new product or system |
| **Prompt Architect** | Creating advanced prompts | Building complex AI prompts |
| **Expert Panel** | Multi-expert discussion | When you need perspectives from different viewpoints |
| **Work Breakdown Structure** | Breaking projects into tasks | Project planning, estimation |

### Steps

**1. Choose an agent for your current problem**

Think about:
- Planning a new project? → **Work Breakdown Structure**
- Need to analyze documents? → **Knowledge Extractor**
- Designing a new product? → **Product Architect**
- Need different perspectives? → **Expert Panel**

**2. Prepare context**

Output quality depends on input quality:
- Describe the problem as specifically as possible
- Attach relevant documents
- Define the expected output

**3. Try the agent**

**Example with Work Breakdown Structure:**

```
I need to break this project into tasks:
- Project: Rolling out AI tools in the marketing department (10 people)
- Deadline: 3 months
- Budget: $5,000 for tools
- Goal: Every team member actively uses AI at least once a day

Create a WBS with:
- Main phases
- Specific tasks and sub-tasks
- Time estimates
- Dependencies
- Milestones
```

**Example with Expert Panel:**

```
I need a discussion about: Should we invest in our own AI solution
or use SaaS tools?

Context: Mid-sized company, 200 employees, IT department of 8 people,
budget $50,000/year for AI.

I want perspectives from: CTO, CFO, CISO, Head of HR, Line Manager.
```

💡 **Tip:** You can use agents in ChatGPT (as GPTs), in Claude (as Projects), or simply paste their instructions into a new chat.

- [ ] Selected agent for a specific managerial task
- [ ] Prepared context and input data
- [ ] Tried the agent on a real problem

## Exercise 7: Evaluate Progress and Plan Ahead

> **💡 Measure what AI has delivered and plan next steps — data will convince even the biggest skeptics.**

**⏱️ 30–45 minutes · Level: Managerial**

**Goal:** Evaluate AI adoption so far, measure the benefits, and create a plan for the next quarter.

### What You'll Do

You'll review the results from all previous exercises, quantify the benefits, and create a plan for the next quarter. By the end, you'll have materials to present to leadership.

### Steps

**1. Evaluate adoption in the team**

- How many people in the team are actively using AI?
- Which tools have taken hold? Which haven't?
- What barriers remain?
- Where are the biggest successes?
- How has your AI champion (from Exercise 3) been involved?

**2. Quantify the benefits**

Try to estimate:
- How many hours per week does AI save individuals?
- How many hours does it save the whole team?
- Has the quality of outputs improved? (fewer errors, faster delivery…)
- What is the return on investment? (licenses vs. saved time)

💡 **Tip:** Even a rough estimate is better than nothing. For example: *"Meeting summaries in Teams Copilot save 15 minutes per meeting × 8 meetings per week × 10 people = 20 hours/week."*

**3. Collect success stories**

Concrete stories are more powerful than numbers:
- Who used AI to solve a problem that would otherwise have taken hours?
- Which tool or process did the team appreciate most?
- What was the biggest "aha" moment?

**4. Create a plan for next quarter**

Based on your experience, define:

**What works — continue:**
- Tools and processes that have proven themselves
- Experience sharing that motivates people

**What doesn't work — adjust:**
- Tools that didn't catch on (why? alternative?)
- Processes that are too complex (simplify?)

**What's missing — add:**
- New tools or licenses
- Training for advanced techniques
- Automation for additional processes

**5. Prepare a presentation for leadership**

Let AI draft the outline:

```
Based on this data, prepare an outline for a 5-minute presentation
to company leadership about AI adoption in our team:

- Number of people actively using AI: [number]
- Estimated savings: [hours/week]
- Most successful tool: [name]
- Biggest success: [story]
- What we need next: [licenses/time/support]

Structure: What we tried → What it delivered → What we're planning next → What we need.
```

### Your Output

- [ ] Overview of adoption in the team (who, what, how)
- [ ] Quantified benefits (hours, quality, ROI)
- [ ] Success stories
- [ ] Plan for next quarter
- [ ] Presentation for leadership
